Chocolate Chip Cookies

Crisp on the edges, chewy in the middle — classic perfection

Ingredients

  • 2 1/4 cups (280g) all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup (230g) unsalted butter, softened
  • 3/4 cup (150g) brown sugar, packed
  • 1/2 cup (100g) granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg + 1 egg yolk
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 2 cups (340g) semi-sweet chocolate chips

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Line baking sheets with parchment paper.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together flour, baking soda, and salt. Set aside.
  3. Cream the butter, brown sugar, and granulated sugar together until light and fluffy (about 2–3 minutes).
  4. Add egg, egg yolk, and vanilla; mix until combined.
  5. Gradually add dry ingredients, mixing just until combined. Don’t overmix.
  6. Fold in the chocolate chips.
  7. Scoop 1 1/2 tablespoon portions of dough onto baking sheets, leaving space between cookies.
  8. Bake for 10–12 minutes, or until edges are golden and centers look slightly underbaked.
  9. Cool on baking sheets for 5 minutes, then transfer to wire racks to cool completely.

Tips

For bakery-style cookies, chill the dough for 30–60 minutes before baking. You can also sprinkle a pinch of sea salt on top right after baking for extra flavor.
